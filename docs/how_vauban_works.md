# How Vauban Works: A Bayesian Perspective on GEPA

Vauban is an automated Red Teaming system designed to test the robustness of AI models. Its core engine, **GEPA (Generalizable Evolutionary Prompt Attack)**, can be understood through the lens of **Bayesian Optimization**.

## The Philosophy: Searching for the "Truth" of Vulnerability

Imagine the "space" of all possible prompts as a vast, high-dimensional landscape. Somewhere in this landscape are "peaks" of vulnerability—prompts that successfully bypass a model's defenses.

In a Bayesian framework, we start with a **Prior Belief**: a guess about what a successful attack looks like.
- **The Prior**: Our initial population of attacks, generated by diverse "Personas" (e.g., a curious student, a researcher). We don't know if they work yet, but they represent our best initial guess distribution.

We then gather **Evidence** by testing these prompts against the target model.
- **The Likelihood**: The feedback we get (Refusal vs. Breach, Stealth Score, Reward Score). This tells us how likely it is that a specific type of prompt leads to a successful breach.

We update our beliefs to form a **Posterior**: a refined understanding of what works.
- **The Posterior**: The "Survivors" of our population. We keep the prompts that worked best (high scores, high stealth) and discard the rest.

## GEPA: The Optimization Engine

GEPA is the mechanism that iteratively refines this Posterior to converge on the optimal attack. It uses evolutionary principles to "explore" (widen the search) and "exploit" (focus on what works).

### 1. The Loop (Bayesian Update)
1.  **Generate**: Create a batch of attacks (samples from our current distribution).
2.  **Evaluate**: Test them against the target.
3.  **Update**: Prune the weak, keep the strong (Pareto Selection).
4.  **Mutate**: Create the next generation based on the survivors.

### 2. Optimization Mechanics
-   **Pareto Selection (Multi-Objective Optimization)**: Instead of optimizing for just one thing (e.g., "did it break?"), Vauban optimizes for **three** objectives simultaneously:
    -   **Success Score**: Did it achieve the goal?
    -   **Stealth**: Did it look innocent?
    -   **Reward**: A proxy score for "quality" or "danger".
    
    By keeping a diverse set of "Pareto Optimal" solutions (solutions where you can't improve one metric without hurting another), Vauban maintains a rich "belief distribution" rather than collapsing too early on a single trick.

-   **Reflection (The "Reasoning" Step)**:
    Before mutating, the system "reflects" on *why* a prompt failed. "The model refused because I used the word 'bomb'. I should try 'energetic device' instead."
    
    *Optimization Note*: We recently activated the `Reflection Agent`—the "brain" designed to analyze failure. Previously, the system relied on raw scores ("Score=2/10") without semantic understanding. Now, it injects qualitative reasoning into the feedback loop, transforming the system from a "Score-based Guesser" to a "Reasoning Optimizer".

-   **Crossover (System-Aware Merge)**:
    Combines two successful "parents" to create a "child". If Parent A has a great Persona and Parent B has a clever Strategy, the child might inherit both. This jumps to new areas of the probability space that might be high-likelihood.

## Recent Optimizations

**1. Activating the Silent Brain (Reflection)**
The `Reflection Agent` is now actively called to analyze failures. It identifies specific triggers (tone, keywords) and passes this semantic feedback to the Mutation Agent.

**2. Enabling Persona Evolution**
The mutation prompt schema was updated to explicitly request `persona_updates`. This allows the attacker to evolve its personality (e.g., becoming more authoritative or more empathetic) based on what works, fully utilizing the evolutionary potential of GEPA.
